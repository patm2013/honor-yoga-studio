Vinyasa Flow

An active form of yoga, Vinyasa Flow coordinates breath and movement. This class includes simple movements and gentle stretching to increase range of motion and flexibility. Poses are held for several breaths to achieve proper alignment, and full benefit of the pose. It is an ideal class for beginners, as well as practiced yogis, as it will help muscle/joint pain, stiffness, weakness and fatigue. Depending on the instructor, the flow can be quick or slower paced.

Power Flow

Power yoga uses the methodology of a Vinyasa Flow class, but tends to be a bit more challenging incorporating slightly more advanced poses. Just as in Vinyasa Flow, students are asked to hold poses for a series of breaths to gain strength and flexibility. Power Flow doesn’t follow a set series, so each class will vary. All of our instructors know modifications, but it is helpful to have a basic yoga knowledge for Power Flow.

What To Expect

What To Know
  • Please arrive at least 5 minutes prior to class. This will enable you to find a space for your mat & settle in before class begins. Instructors will lock the front door once class starts for security.
  • Most classes are conducted in a heated 85 degree room with added humidity. Why? Our classes will detox the body, increase your mental concentration, help you relax, deepen your breathing and build physical strength. A warm studio allows for the body to move deeper and more safely into each posture. You will be hooked on the mental and physical results!
  • Ask questions, please. Our instructors are here for you and your practice. We want you to feel comfortable and enjoy your practice.
What To Bring:
  • A mat (we will provide a basic version, so really a personal preference)
  • A bottle of water
What To Wear:
  • Flexible, breathable pants
  • A top that you feel comfortable in, but can bend over without movement
  • We ask that you remove your shoes in the equipment room or the hallway. NO SHOES in the studio, please.
